<objective>
Integrate `ModelsLab/omnivoice-singing` (SPIKE-02 = GO with reduced scope per `.planning/phases/04-adaptive-specialty-engines-spike-first/04-RESEARCH.md`) as a singing-finetune subclass of the existing `OmniVoiceBackend`, extend the dubbing pipeline with a "singing mode" toggle that routes vocal-stem segments through the singing engine while preserving the instrumental stem, ship the SING-03 pitch-stability + energy heuristic segment detector on the Demucs vocal stem with per-segment user override, and ratify the decision in `.planning/decisions/SPIKE-02-singing.md`.

Purpose: Sung segments of dubbed content currently produce speech-like output even when the source is music or musical theatre. Routing those segments through a finetune trained on tagged singing data fixes the loudest unaddressed dubbing complaint without adding any new Python dependency — the same `omnivoice` PyPI library already shipping handles the new model ID.

Output: 6 SING-* requirements implemented + SPIKE-02 ADR ratified with the SING-02 scope decision recorded.

**Wave 2 ordering note (per RESEARCH.md Open Question #4 + GO conditions):** Task 1 of this plan is a code-read of `backend/services/dub_pipeline.py` that decides SING-02's scope (per-segment routing vs whole-job toggle). The remaining tasks are sized against the smaller of the two outcomes; if the code-read finds the larger refactor is required, Task 2's `<action>` block explicitly downscopes to a whole-job toggle for v0.3 and tracks per-segment routing for v0.4.
